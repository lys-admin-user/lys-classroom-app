 Character, Values, and Principles. 
 E ducation is rooted in relationships. You cannot teach that which is inanimate. The teacher is critical to the student. So much so, that the student should always have a teacher. The responsibility of finding and having the provision of a perpetual teacher could be likened to the never-ending trail in Alice in Wonderland. Thus, intermittent and iterative mentorship is viable and achievable. 
 The student needs to: 1) know holistic fundamentals, 2) Know how they learn best, 3) Know how to select a mentor for the need, 4) be open to life providing the teacher. 
 The student teacher relationship is sacred. A teacher provides a curriculum. Life does as well. 
 The existential disciplines such as philosophy, religion, and economics are universal for a reason. 
 Character in its existential sense is knowing what makes you alive. 
 Students understanding their own values helps them to sort out their virtue and vice on their journey towards actualization. 
 Principles ground and guide the conscious learner in times of uncertainty as the gaps of instruction are inevitable.

 Knowing 
 Change is the only constant. In our technological age, change requires unabated personal adaptability. 
 Knowing as substantive reality. 
 Focus is What is (essence) and How does (function) 
 Try to know the substance of a thing or situation. 
 Cut to the chase and get to the heart of a matter. 
 Seek to know the elemental aspects of information, situations, and people as well as connections or patterns 
 “I did then what I knew how to do. Now that I know better, I do better.”  ―  Maya Angelou 

 The next critical question worth knowing is how. How leads to action or is derived from the desire to act. 
 In most formal or academic settings, how is accomplished by following the directions, protocols, and instructions given to tell us how to do a thing. But, how is not always clear. 
 How is something that can be maximized. For example, in any college graduating class roughly 50% of students will not work in the field that they graduated in. Which means that the students that did graduate and get a job in their field of study somehow made the best out of a rather mundane occurrence. Maximizing the how occurs when we identify our own intentions, potential, resources, and possibility. This is done both internally and externally.

 Doing 
 Character should be exercised here. 
 Help the student develop their own sense of planning. 
 Take into account both the learner’s intention and the intention of the educator. 
 What are the best planning methodologies? 
 Development of processes for being consistent and disciplined. 
 If we parse the Latin etymology of education, we find the words uplift caution or care. A good education constantly uplifts the concerns of the learner bringing some very real sense of closure. 
 “The goal of education is to enable individuals to continue their education.”  ―  John Dewey 
 There are seven key domains in which being, knowing, and doing operate or are expressed. Those domains are: education, cultural, health, financial, spiritual, vocational, and social. These domains are immutable as they structure and society. 
 If we empower the student in their being, knowing, and doing we can create lifelong learners that in turn help to forge a more productive society. 
 The beginning of individualized learning is understanding that we can’t control all the factors that encompass the learner.
