 Lesson objectives:  

 By the end of the lesson, students will be able to develop drafts into focused, structured, and coherent pieces of writing under timed conditions. 

 Students will use this information to write an in-class essay. 
 By the end of the lesson, students will be able to organizing structure appropriate to the purpose, audience, topic, and context. 

 Students will use this information to revise their short story. 
 By the end of the lesson, students will be able to develop an engaging idea that reflects depth of thought, supported by specific facts, details, examples, and commentary.

 Students will use this information to revise their short story with an emphasis on supporting details. 
 By the end of the lesson, students will be able to revise drafts for clarity, development, organization, style, word choice, and sentence effectiveness. 

 Students will use this information to revise their short story with an emphasis on clarity. 
 By the end of the lesson, students will be able to edit drafts using standard English conventions. 

 Students will use this information to edit their short story for spelling and grammar.

 What is the answer to the essential question, and why does it matter to me in terms of: 
 Educational 
 Being a student requires more than simply memorizing information for tests; it requires a certain degree of educational curiosity. What are some topics you are educationally curious about? What do you think it means to be educationally curious? 

 Financial 
 Being successful in school requires you to develop reading and writing skills that you can use to pursue your education and a vocation. In addition to reading skills, you will need financial skills to be successful. What do you think are the most important financial skills you need ito surviveyour own personalboom-and-bust cycless? 

 Social 
 As you go through life, you will meet lots of new people. Some of these people will come in and out of your life. While people come and go, people you can trust (and feel they can trust you) tend to stick around more. How can you learn to trust someone? What are some indicators of when you can trust somebody? 
 Lesson Close 

 What is the answer to the essential question, and why does it matter to me in terms of: 
 Spiritual 
 All of us will face trials and tribulations in our lives. When we face difficult times, especially ones that might be traumatic, it is important to hold onto hope- the belief that things will get better. What are some ways you can hold onto hope? 

 Health 
 An important aspect of maintaining good health is keeping your health in balance- having too many or not enough healthy habits can actually have serious repercussions for your health. Knowing how important it is to keep your health in balance, what are some simple ways to help keep your health in balance? 

 Vocational 
 Professionalism is defined as the competence or skill expected of a professional. What this means is that as you develop skills and in education, your coworkers and customers will expect your ability to do your job to increase. For example, if you are a doctor, you'll be expected to perform basic medical procedures, such as taking blood pressure. However, as you progress in your medical career, you will be expected to handle increasingly complex tasks. Regardless of your career choice or where you start, your professionalism is directly tied to using the skills and education required for someone with your level of experience. How can you increase your professionalism at work? 
 Lesson Close 

 What is the answer to the essential question, and why does it matter to me in terms of: 
 Social 
 Empathy, which involves understanding someone else's feelings, is not easy for everyone. Some of us struggle with empathy, and others are naturally empathetic. Regardless of how empathetic you are, there is always room for improvement. What are some ways that you can improve your empathy? 

 Financial 
 Many people make a living by selling products online through personal stores such as Etsy. When you buy goods and services from stores like this, it is important to know the material value of a good to avoid being overcharged. What are simple ways you can determine the material value of something? 

 Spiritual 
 One of the great mysteries of life is wondering why we are here and what the meaning of our lives is. To seek answers to these questions, people rely on a wide variety of sources, including their spirituality. How can you use your spirituality to help you find meaning in your life? 
 Lesson Close 

 What is the answer to the essential question, and why does it matter to me in terms of: 
 Education 
 Many students dislike taking tests and quizzes or partaking in any type of assessment. This is perfectly understandable. Many students don't realize that teachers use these assessments to measure their educational competence. If you feel that traditional assessments, such as tests or quizzes, cannot allow you to demonstrate your competence properly, what are some non-traditional ways that you can demonstrate your educational competence? 

 Social 
 Social skills did not come easily for everybody. Some people are inherently social, while others need to put in a lot of effort to develop social skills. An easy way of developing your social skills is forming a basic connection with someone- even if you are super shy. What are some easy ways you can connect with someone? 

 Educational 
 While in school, many of the things you learn are skills you use throughout your life. For example, while you might hate the books that teachers assign to you, or you might hate writing persuasive essays, the skills that you rely on to complete these assignments can help you outside of school. For example, developing your reading comprehension by reading a book you don’t like can help you better understand a book you do like, and learning how to write a persuasive essay and build an argument might help you be more persuasive in arguments and discussions with others. How can you use your educational skills outside of the classroom? 
 Lesson Close 

 What is the answer to the essential question, and why does it matter to me in terms of: 
 Cultural 
 Humans, regardless of where they live, tend to form a community. Whether it is your school, your workplace, or the town you live in, all of these elements make up your local community. Think about the people around you. What makes up your community? 

 Educational 
 Each vocation requires a different series of degrees. For example, if you want to work in the medical field, the type of job you wish to hold will determine the degree you need. For example, if you want to become a medical doctor, you will need to earn a Doctor of Medicine degree. If you want to be a nurse or technician, you might only need an Associate's Degree. Think about the types of vocations you are interested in pursuing. What types of degrees do you need to be successful in your chosen vocation? 

 Vocational 
 As with any profession, being a professional artist requires a high level of productivity. Since many of these artists are paid by the work rather than by a fixed salary, their ability to make a living is directly tied to how many works they can sell in a given year. While most jobs are salary-based rather than commission-based, all jobs require you to be productive. However, this doesn’t mean you need to work around the clock and not take any time to create a work-life balance. After all, working all the time will leave you with little time for friends, family, and yourself, which can, in turn, leave you burned out and unproductive. Knowing that productivity needs to be delicately balanced, how can you be productive without damaging your work-life balance?.
