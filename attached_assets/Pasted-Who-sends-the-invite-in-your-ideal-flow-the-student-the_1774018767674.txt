Who sends the invite in your ideal flow — the student, the teacher, or the campus admin? 
I think it’s important to be as flexible as possible with the parent being able to invite the student if they are the first user/to discover the app. Even if they are not homeschooling and they just want to use the app for the other features, it’s 1000% more important if they are homeschooling. 
I think that teachers and campus administrators should be able to invite parents as well.
Do you want parents to need a Replit account, or should there be a lighter-weight access (like a PIN or magic link they click to view a read-only dashboard)?
There should definitely be a way to invite outside of Replit and also a lightweight way that they can connect via a PIN or Magic link, similar to the affiliate model that we already have. But they should not get any points for adding a family member.
Should teachers be able to see the parent-student connection, or is that purely private between family and student?
Yes. They should also be able to give feedback.
We should consider the platform that facilitates a tripartite interaction model focused on asynchronous feedback, centralized communication, and digital portfolios. It employs a real-time token economy where administrators push positive reinforcement notifications to student clients, which are simultaneously logged for parent oversight. Communication is bifurcated into a one-to-many broadcast feed for classroom-wide transparency and a secure one-to-one messaging layer with integrated localization tools and boundary-setting features like "Quiet Hours." Finally, the system utilizes a mediated content-sharing workflow, allowing students to upload artifacts to a personal gallery that remains invisible to parents until a moderator (the teacher) validates the entry, ensuring a controlled yet collaborative feedback loop.
What notifications matter most — milestone completions, low engagement alerts, new portfolio items, assignment grades?
I think we should have a basic dashboard with all the information and they should be able to toggle on and off what they do not want to see.
