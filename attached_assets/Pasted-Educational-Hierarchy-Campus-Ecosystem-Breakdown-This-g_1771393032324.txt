Educational Hierarchy & Campus Ecosystem Breakdown

This guide breaks down the different educational structures, their decision-making power, and the web of connected organizations that utilize the LYS ecosystem.

1. Internal Campus Teams (The "Engine")

These are the sub-groups within a school that use the app to coordinate daily operations.

Grade Level Teams: Use the app for "Horizontal Alignment." (e.g., All 9th-grade teachers ensuring they are hitting the same TEKS at the same time).

Content/Department Teams: Use the app for "Vertical Alignment." (e.g., The Science Dept. ensuring 6th-grade "Matter" lessons properly prepare students for 8th-grade "Chemistry").

SPED & ESL Support Staff: Use the "Auto Assignment" modification feature to adapt general education "Bricks" for students with IEPs or language barriers.

Counseling & CCMR Office: The primary users of the "Predictive Analytics" (Phase 2) to identify students who are ready for specific career or college ladders.

2. External Connected Organizations (The "Partners")

These organizations plug into the campus via the "Parent/Mentor" role in the LYS App.

Non-Profit & Youth Mentorship Orgs: (e.g., Boys & Girls Club, local church youth groups). They use the app's communication suite to track student "Success" metrics and reinforce the "Being" methodology outside of school hours.

Workforce Development Boards: Connect to high school campuses to provide "Doing" milestones (internships, certifications) that align with local labor data (BLS/OECD).

Higher Ed / HBCU Partners: Use the "Success" data to identify potential scholarship candidates early (9th or 10th grade) rather than waiting for graduation.

After-School Enrichment Providers: Organizations teaching specialized skills (coding, urban farming, music) contribute their own "Bricks" to the Campus Library.

3. The LYS "NEST" Site Integration (Global/Study Abroad)

For schools participating in the LYS global mission, the campus extends across borders.

Partner Schools Abroad: A campus in Houston may be digitally linked to a "NEST" site in Lagos. Students collaborate on the same "Knowing" bricks but apply them to different "Doing" projects in their respective countries.

Local Small Businesses: Acts as "Vocation Mentors." They can provide feedback on a student's "Doing" assignment, effectively acting as an external grader for career-ready skills.
