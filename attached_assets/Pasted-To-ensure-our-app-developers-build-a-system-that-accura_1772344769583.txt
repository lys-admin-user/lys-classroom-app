To ensure our app developers build a system that accurately reflects the educational landscape, we need to categorize schools based on their Governance, Funding, and Operational Scale. Here is a summary of the three primary "User Types" or "Client Structures" the developers need to account for:1. Multi-State Charter Networks (CMOs/EMOs)The Model: Think of these as "Educational Franchises." A central headquarters (The CMO/EMO) manages dozens or hundreds of schools across different states (e.g., KIPP, IDEA).Dev Priority (Centralization): The app needs a Master Admin Dashboard. A change in curriculum or a hiring policy at the HQ level must be able to "push" updates to all campuses across multiple states simultaneously.Data Structure: Needs to handle state-specific compliance (e.g., Florida vs. Texas laws) within a single unified organization.Key Entities: Non-profits (CMOs) like Green Dot or For-profits (EMOs) like Charter Schools USA.2. Traditional Public School Districts (ISDs)The Model: Geographically bound and locally governed by an elected board. These are the "Legacy Systems."Dev Priority (Regulation & Tax): These entities have the power to levy local taxes. The app must handle complex integration with traditional state education agency reporting and union-regulated staffing rules.Data Structure: High standardization. All schools in the district typically follow the exact same calendar, payroll, and curriculum.3. Single-Campus ChartersThe Model: Independent, "Boutique" schools. They are often "mom-and-pop" versions of charters, serving one specific community or niche (e.g., a single STEM-focused school).Dev Priority (Flexibility): These users need the highest level of customization. They don't have a "corporate HQ" pushing settings to them; they make their own rules for their specific site.Data Structure: Individualized.
