Overview

The Country Affordability Index (CAI) is a proprietary scoring system developed by The Nomad Network to enable equitable, cross-border pricing for healthcare services, consulting, education, and business transactions. The CAI ensures that pricing reflects true local purchasing power rather than simple currency conversion.

The Problem CAI Solves

Foreign exchange (FX) conversion alone does not equal affordability.

A service priced at $100 USD may be affordable in the United States but represent a significant financial burden in developing countries, even after currency conversion. The CAI addresses this by adjusting prices based on local economic realities.

CAI Score Calculation

Each country receives a CAI Score ranging from 0.05 to 1.0, derived from four weighted factors:

Factor

Weight

Description

GDP per Capita (PPP)

35%

Purchasing power parity-adjusted gross domestic product per person

25%

Average monthly earnings for working-age population

Out-of-Pocket Healthcare Spend

Urban Cost-of-Living Index

Local Cost-of-Services Index (LCSI)

The LCSI is an additional adjustment factor (0 to 0.15) that accounts for:

Regional cost variations within countries
Local healthcare infrastructure costs
Professional service availability premiums
Urban vs. rural pricing differences
The CAI-FX Pricing Formula

Recommended Local Price = Global Reference Price x (CAI Score + LCSI Adjustment)
